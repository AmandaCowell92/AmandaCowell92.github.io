Piano Lab Pro

Mixed reality performance preparation on Apple Vision Pro. Built at the University of Michigan's Center for Academic Innovation.

The problem

Music students rehearse in practice rooms but perform in concert halls. The acoustic and psychological gap between the two is large, and traditional practice doesn't close it. Students arrive at recitals underprepared for the specific conditions they'll face: the venue acoustics, the audience presence, the stakes.

What I built

A mixed reality app on Apple Vision Pro that lets music students rehearse under authentic performance conditions:

  • 3D-modeled U-M concert venues so students practice in the actual spaces they'll perform in
  • Simulated audience distractions varied across randomized conditions
  • A perception-based self-scoring system that captures anxiety, experience level, performance perception, and coping reflection

The decision I'm proudest of

It wasn't a feature. It was identifying a capability gap early.

Our team at CAI didn't have objective impact measurement expertise in-house, and self-reported anxiety scores weren't going to be enough to validate whether the app actually worked. I sought out a partnership with U-M's Institute for Social Research to bring biometric validation into the project. That partnership reshaped how we'd eventually measure success at scale, and it came from a read on what the team couldn't do alone, not from a feature spec.

What I learned

Scoping three competing faculty concepts down to one differentiated value proposition is a PM decision that looks like a writing exercise until you've tried it. The hardest part is saying no to ideas that are good on their own when you're narrowing for the strongest product thesis. That skill translates directly to client work.
